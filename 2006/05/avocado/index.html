Avocado

Mon, May 1, 2006 music review, pearl jam

Pearl Jam’s new album is officially released, Tuesday May 2nd. I received my pre-ordered copy Friday. Thanks, Ten Club.

It’s a really good album. Upon first listen I thought Riot Act was a much better ablum, and wasn’t sure why this one was so over-hyped. Riot Act IS a great ablum, which doesn’t get the respect it deserves, but this self-titled album is really good too. It is growing on me.

I wasn’t sure about “Marker in the Sand” at first, but now I think it’s my favorite song on the album. “Come Back”, “Gone”, and “Inside Job” are all great songs. “Life Wasted” is my favorite of the heavier rock stuff, and the reprise is cool too.

Initially I thought the album kinda lulled in the middle, but “Marker In The Sand”, “Unemployable” and “Gone” are all great. “Big Wave” is my least favorite song on this release.

I’m still not sure what’s with all the hype, about this being their “best album in ten years“, or “return to eminence” (This guy also says it’s their first studio release since 2000, so he doesn’t even count Riot Act). To me it sounds pretty much like Pearl Jam has since Matt Cameron joined (although ‘Binaural’ sounded much better live than in album form, to me at least). It’s not amazingly better than ‘Riot Act’, not at all, and it doesn’t sound like Vs. or Ten.

All in all it’s a good album, from a band that has continuously put out good albums. I can’t wait to see them perform these songs live in a couple weeks.
